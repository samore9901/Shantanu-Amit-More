Series-Parallel Hybrid Powertrain Modeling & Supervisory Control

SimulinkStateflow
▾
Situation
A hybrid powertrain has to split power between an engine and an electric motor to maximize fuel economy without breaking battery or drivetrain limits.
Task
Size the powertrain components and design the control logic that decides, moment to moment, where torque comes from.
Action
  • Sized the electric motor and internal combustion engine from torque–speed curves and efficiency contour maps.
  • Modeled a series-parallel hybrid powertrain architecture in Simulink.
  • Designed rule-based supervisory control logic in Stateflow that allocates torque between power sources based on battery SoC, vehicle speed, and torque demand.
Result
Benchmarked the architecture against pure-ICE and pure-EV powertrains over a city drive cycle, maximizing fuel economy while staying inside every operating limit.
/ 02

Vehicle Stability Control for Independent-Wheel-Drive EVs

MATLABSimulinkCarSimMPC
▾
Situation
A four-wheel-independent-drive EV can lose yaw stability and spin out on low-adhesion surfaces if torque isn't allocated intelligently across all four motors.
Task
Build a controller that tracks yaw rate, limits sideslip, and allocates torque optimally across all four motors in real time.
Action
  • Developed a hierarchical controller combining Mamdani fuzzy logic with Model Predictive Control on a control-oriented state-space model.
  • Enforced torque, torque-rate, and tire-slip constraints directly in the controller design.
  • Validated the controller via software-in-the-loop co-simulation in MATLAB/Simulink and CarSim across double-lane-change and sine-wave-steer maneuvers.
Result
Cut low-adhesion yaw-rate divergence from ~70°/s to ±12°/s while keeping sideslip near zero, preventing spin-out. At 100 km/h sine-wave steer, reduced peak yaw rate by 30–45% and peak sideslip by ~75%.
/ 03

Engine Dyno Data Analysis, Calibration & Testing

MATLABLabVIEW TDMSDyno Testing
▾
Situation
Calibrating spark-ignition and diesel engines for efficiency and emissions requires turning raw dyno and combustion data into usable calibration boundaries.
Task
Analyze combustion behavior, knock, and cycle-to-cycle variation on a 2.3 L turbo-gasoline and a 13 L diesel engine to guide spark, injection, and knock-limit calibration.
Action
  • Calibrated spark timing across load and air-fuel-ratio sweeps using heat-release-rate and CA10/50/90 combustion phasing to locate MBT timing.
  • Optimized diesel injection strategy across start-of-injection, rail-pressure, and speed sweeps, correlating combustion phasing and ignition delay against NOx and CO.
  • Ran FFT-based knock analysis on in-cylinder pressure and quantified cycle-to-cycle stability via COV of IMEP and peak pressure.
  • Processed high-speed dyno and LabVIEW TDMS data in MATLAB to compute IMEP, BMEP, FMEP, and brake/indicated fuel-conversion efficiency.
  • Developed and validated a zero-dimensional Wiebe combustion model against experimental cylinder-pressure and mass-fraction-burned data.
Result
Reached a 36.6% peak indicated efficiency at MBT timing, identified knock frequencies above 5 kHz to set spark-retard limits, and mapped emissions-vs-efficiency tradeoffs across both engine platforms.

/ 05

Electric Machine & Battery Cell Modeling Studies

MATLABSimulinkANSYS Maxwell
▾
Situation
Electric and hybrid powertrains depend on accurately modeled motors and battery cells before any controller can be designed around them.
Task
Build first-principles models of a permanent-magnet synchronous motor, an induction machine, and a Li-ion cell to characterize their electrical and electromagnetic behavior.
Action
  • PMSMSimulated differential-equation-based Field-Oriented Control to evaluate motor-performance sensitivity to machine parameters, then modeled the motor in ANSYS Maxwell to extract electromagnetic output parameters.
  • INDUCTIONDerived differential-equation energy-conversion models for a linear electromagnetic machine and simulated a 3-phase induction machine using reference-frame theory, testing power and losses across load conditions.
  • CELLBuilt a Simulink 2-RC equivalent-circuit model of a Li-ion cell to estimate state of charge from its open-circuit-voltage-vs-SoC curve.
Result
Produced validated, parameter-sensitive models of both machine types and the cell — the same modeling foundation reused for the FOC, supervisory-control, and Kalman-filter SoC-estimation work elsewhere in this portfolio.
